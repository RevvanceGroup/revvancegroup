Our Story

The Chef

Chef Maria Castellano

Maria Castellano grew up in her grandmother's kitchen in Tuscany, where she learned that the best food begins with the best ingredients and a generous spirit. After culinary school in Bologna, she spent a decade honing her craft in Michelin-starred kitchens across Rome, Paris, and New York.

But something was missing. The connection between farmer and table, between soil and plate, had been lost in the pursuit of perfection. In 2018, Maria returned to Asheville with co-founder James Whitfield and a bold idea: build a restaurant where every ingredient comes from within 50 miles, where the farmer is as important as the chef.

The Rustic Table was born not just from culinary ambition, but from a deep belief that food tastes better when you know where it comes from and who grew it.

— Chef Maria Castellano

Our Philosophy

Every Ingredient Has a Story

We believe in the power of proximity. When your tomatoes ripen just 12 miles away, your beef is raised on pastures you can visit, and your cheese is crafted by hands you know by name, something remarkable happens. The food becomes more than a meal. It becomes a conversation between land and table, farmer and guest.

Our Partners

The Farms Behind Our Food

We visit every farm, know every farmer, and taste every ingredient before it appears on your plate. These are the partners who make our vision possible.

Meadow Creek Farms

18 miles away

Organic seasonal vegetables, heirloom tomatoes, and fresh herbs grown using regenerative agriculture practices. The Henderson family has farmed this land for three generations.

Heritage Hill Ranch

32 miles away

Grass-fed, pasture-raised beef and lamb. Their cattle graze on mountain pastures year-round, resulting in rich, complex flavors that define our signature dishes.

Sunrise Dairy

24 miles away

Small-batch artisan cheeses and fresh dairy. Their award-winning aged cheddar and creamy burrata are staples on our menu throughout the year.

Transparency

From Soil to Plate

We believe you deserve to know where your food comes from. That is why we list our farm partners on our menu, invite guests to visit our partner farms, and change our dishes with the seasons rather than relying on industrial supply chains.

Every decision we make is guided by one question: does this honor the ingredient, the farmer, and the guest?
