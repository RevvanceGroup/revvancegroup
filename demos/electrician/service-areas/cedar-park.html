Cedar Park FAQ

Common Questions from Cedar Park Homeowners

My Cedar Park home was built around 2012 — do I need a panel upgrade for an EV charger?
Most Cedar Park homes from 2005–2020 came with 200A panels, which typically have capacity for one Level 2 EV charger without an upgrade. However, if you're already running a high-load HVAC system, an electric range, and a hot tub, adding a 50-amp EV circuit may require a load calculation to confirm. We do that calculation as part of any EV charger estimate at no extra charge.
Do you pull permits through the City of Cedar Park or Williamson County?
Depending on the location in Cedar Park, permits are pulled through either the City of Cedar Park's building department or Williamson County. We determine the correct jurisdiction for your address and handle all permit applications and inspection scheduling on your behalf.
Can I get an Austin Energy EV charger rebate if I live in Cedar Park?
Cedar Park is served by Bluebonnet Electric, not Austin Energy, so the Austin Energy EV rebate program does not apply to Cedar Park addresses. Bluebonnet Electric does offer its own incentive programs for EV charger installation — we can advise you on current eligibility when we scope your project.
How far is Voltage Electric from Cedar Park?
Our shop is at 2847 Industrial Blvd in South Austin — roughly a 30 to 40 minute drive to most Cedar Park neighborhoods via 183 or 183A. We schedule Cedar Park jobs regularly throughout the week and can typically accommodate same-week non-emergency scheduling.
