Pflugerville FAQ

Common Questions from Pflugerville Homeowners

My Pflugerville home was built around 2002 — do I likely have a 100A or 200A panel?
Homes built in Pflugerville between roughly 1995 and 2005 are a mixed bag — some builders used 100A panels to keep costs down, while others installed 150A or 200A service. The only way to know for sure is to look at the main breaker rating on your panel. We check this as part of any estimate visit and will tell you exactly what you have and what it means for your planned project.
Do you need to upgrade my panel before installing an EV charger?
Not necessarily. We do a load calculation based on your current panel size and existing loads. Many Pflugerville homeowners with 200A panels have enough headroom for a 50-amp EV circuit without any panel work. If your panel is 100A and already running a full household load, we'll explain your options clearly before recommending anything.
Which permitting office handles Pflugerville electrical permits?
Pflugerville is primarily in Travis County, though some annexed areas have their own permitting through the City of Pflugerville. We determine the correct jurisdiction for your address and handle all permit applications and scheduling. You won't need to contact any permitting office yourself.
How quickly can you get to Pflugerville for an emergency?
We offer 24/7 emergency electrical service throughout the Austin metro including Pflugerville. For true electrical emergencies — burning smell, sparking, sudden unexplained power loss — call us directly at (512) 847-4200. Response times vary with traffic and current job load, but we treat electrical emergencies with the urgency they require.
