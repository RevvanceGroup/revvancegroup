Round Rock FAQ

Common Questions from Round Rock Homeowners

My 1978 Round Rock home may have aluminum wiring — what should I do?
Aluminum branch circuit wiring was common in homes built between 1965 and 1973, but some builders continued using it into the late 1970s. We can inspect your panel and outlets to determine whether aluminum wiring is present. Options range from installing CO/ALR-rated devices at each outlet, to pigtailing with copper at every connection, to full rewiring. We'll explain all options with honest cost information so you can make the right decision for your situation.
Can my older Round Rock home support an EV charger without a panel upgrade?
It depends on your current panel amperage and what loads are already on it. Homes from the 1970s and 80s often have 100A panels, which may not have enough capacity for a 50-amp EV circuit without upgrades. We'll do a load calculation as part of any EV charger estimate and tell you exactly what your panel can support before you commit to anything.
