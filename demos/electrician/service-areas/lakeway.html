Lakeway FAQ

Common Questions from Lakeway Homeowners

What size generator do I need for my Lakeway home?
Generator sizing depends on what loads you want to protect. A 22kW air-cooled generator handles most single-family homes including central HVAC, refrigerators, lighting, and basic appliances. Larger Lakeway properties with multiple HVAC zones, pool equipment, and other heavy loads may need a 26kW or liquid-cooled unit. We do a load assessment as part of every generator estimate and recommend the right size — not the largest one we can sell you.
Can you install outdoor lighting near my pool or dock?
Yes. Outdoor electrical work near water requires specific weatherproof fixtures, GFCI protection, and proper conduit installation — all of which are covered by NEC code. We're experienced with pool deck, patio, and dock lighting in the Lakeway area and will ensure everything is installed to code and properly permitted.
Does my Lakeway home need a 400-amp panel?
Most Lakeway homes do well on 200A service unless you're running multiple large HVAC systems, a pool pump, an EV charger, a hot tub, and a generator — all simultaneously at peak load. We'll do a load calculation for your property and give you an honest answer. Some larger Lakeway homes genuinely need 400A; others don't. We won't recommend an upgrade you don't need.
Do you work with custom home builders in Lakeway?
Yes. We work with custom and semi-custom home builders on new construction electrical throughout the Lakeway and Lake Travis area. Rough-in wiring, panel installation, trim-out, smart home pre-wiring, and all specialty circuits. Contact us to discuss your build timeline and scope.
