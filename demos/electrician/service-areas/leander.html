Leander FAQ

Common Questions from Leander Homeowners

My new Leander home has a 200A panel but I want to add two EV chargers — is that possible?
In most cases, yes. Two 50-amp EV circuits draw 100 amps combined at full load, which is a significant portion of a 200A panel's capacity. Whether your panel can support both chargers without issue depends on the existing load — HVAC, electric range, water heater, and other large appliances. We do a load calculation as part of every EV charger estimate. Many Leander homeowners can add two circuits without a panel upgrade; some need to consider a panel upgrade or load management equipment. We'll give you an honest assessment.
Do you work with Leander's permitting office for new construction?
Yes. Most Leander new construction permitting goes through the City of Leander's building department or Williamson County depending on whether the property is within city limits or ETJ. We work with both regularly and handle all permit applications, inspections scheduling, and code compliance documentation for your project.
How long is the drive from your shop to Leander?
Our shop is at 2847 Industrial Blvd in South Austin. Leander is roughly 35 to 50 minutes via 183A depending on traffic and which part of Leander you're in. We schedule Leander jobs regularly and can typically get out within the week for non-emergency work. For emergencies, call us directly at (512) 847-4200.
Can you pre-wire a new Leander home for future EV charging even if I don't have an EV yet?
Absolutely, and it's a smart move. Running conduit and pulling a 50-amp circuit to the garage while walls are still open during construction costs a fraction of what it costs to add it later. We can pre-wire for one or two future EV circuits, cap them off neatly in the panel and at the garage wall, and you're ready to plug in whenever the time comes. This is a popular option for Leander new construction projects.
