Panel Services

Panel Upgrades & Replacements

Your electrical panel is the heart of your home's electrical system. If your home still has 100-amp service, a Federal Pacific Stab-Lok panel, or a Zinsco breaker box, you're carrying real safety and insurance risk. These panels have documented failure rates and are flagged by home inspectors and insurance underwriters across Texas.

Voltage Electric specializes in panel replacements and service upgrades. We upgrade 100A service to 200A — which is now the minimum recommended capacity for modern homes with EV chargers, HVAC systems, and whole-home entertainment setups. We also install whole-home surge protectors at the panel to protect your appliances and electronics from voltage spikes during Austin's frequent storm activity.

All panel work is done with permits pulled before we start and a city inspection completed before we close up. You receive a final inspection report for your records and insurance provider.

  • 100A to 200A service upgrade — handles modern electrical loads
  • Federal Pacific Stab-Lok panel replacement — eliminates documented fire risk
  • Zinsco / Sylvania panel replacement — corrects breaker failure issues
  • Whole-home surge protection installed at the main panel
  • Sub-panel installation for garages, workshops, and additions
  • Full permit pulled and city inspection scheduled and completed

EV Charging

EV Charger Installation

Austin leads Texas in EV adoption, and a Level 2 home charger is no longer a luxury — it's the most practical way to own an electric vehicle. Unlike a standard 120V outlet that adds only 3-5 miles of range per hour, a properly installed 240V Level 2 charger delivers 20-30 miles of range per hour, meaning your vehicle is fully charged overnight every time.

We install all major Level 2 home charging equipment including Tesla Wall Connectors, ChargePoint, JuiceBox, and Enel X JuiceBox. Before installing, we evaluate your panel capacity and determine whether a panel upgrade is needed to support dedicated 50-amp EV charging circuits. Many homes in Austin built before 2010 require a small panel upgrade or a dedicated circuit addition before an EV charger can be safely added.

Every EV charger installation includes permit filing, city inspection scheduling, and a 2-year workmanship warranty. We also provide a load calculation and written documentation if you're applying for a utility rebate through Austin Energy.

  • Tesla Wall Connector, ChargePoint, JuiceBox, Enel X installation
  • Dedicated 240V/50A circuit installation — correct gauge wire, proper breaker
  • Panel capacity evaluation — no surprises after the work is done
  • Permit pulled and city electrical inspection completed
  • Garage and carport mounting — weatherproof outdoor installations
  • Austin Energy rebate documentation provided upon request

Backup Power

Generator Installation

February 2021 reminded every Austin homeowner that the power grid can fail when you need it most. A whole-home standby generator means your family never faces a days-long blackout again — heat, refrigerator, medical equipment, and lights stay on automatically, within seconds of an outage, without you having to do anything.

Voltage Electric is an authorized Generac and Kohler dealer. We size generators correctly for your home's actual load — most Austin homes with HVAC, well pumps, or medical equipment need a 22kW or larger unit, and undersizing is a common mistake that leaves critical circuits unpowered during emergencies. We handle the full installation including the automatic transfer switch, natural gas or propane line connection, and all required permits.

After installation, we register your warranty with the manufacturer and schedule your first annual maintenance visit. Generac's 5-year limited residential warranty applies to all new units we install.

Rewiring

Whole-Home Rewiring

Older Austin homes — particularly those built before 1975 — often carry wiring that poses genuine hazards. Knob-and-tube wiring, a fabric-insulated system that was standard in homes built before the 1940s, was never designed for modern electrical loads and has lost its insulating properties with age. Aluminum wiring, widely used in homes built from the mid-1960s through the 1970s, expands and contracts at a different rate than the outlet and switch connections it terminates in — creating loose connections that arc, overheat, and start fires.

We perform complete whole-home rewiring with modern copper conductors, updated outlet boxes, and a new panel if required. Our process is designed to minimize disruption — we work methodically room by room and restore power at the end of each day. All rewiring work is done to the current National Electrical Code (NEC) and includes a full inspection by the City of Austin.

  • Knob-and-tube wiring removal and replacement — full code compliance
  • Aluminum wiring remediation — CO/ALR devices or full replacement
  • Modern copper wiring with arc-fault and ground-fault protection
  • New outlet, switch, and junction boxes installed throughout
  • Panel upgrade included when required for code compliance
  • Homeowner works with insurance — we provide all documentation needed

Emergency Service

Emergency Electrical Repairs

Electrical emergencies don't keep business hours. A repeatedly tripping breaker, a burning smell from your panel, outlets that go dead without explanation, or visible sparking at a switch or outlet all require immediate attention — not an appointment next Tuesday. These symptoms can indicate arc faults, failing breakers, overloaded circuits, or damaged wiring that poses a serious fire risk.

Our emergency line is answered by a live electrician — not a call center — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. We can typically have a licensed technician at your door within 60 minutes anywhere in the Austin metro area. We'll diagnose the issue, give you a firm price before touching anything, and get your system safe and operational that night.

Common emergency calls we handle: breaker that won't reset, power out in part of the house, GFCIs tripping repeatedly, burning or melting smell near outlets or panels, outlet stopped working after a storm, and flickering lights on a whole circuit.

  • 24/7 live answer — a licensed electrician picks up, not a voicemail

  • Upfront pricing before work begins — even on emergency calls
