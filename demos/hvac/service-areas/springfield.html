Springfield Residents Have Trusted Meridian Since 2011

Meridian started taking Springfield calls in 2011 when a handful of our Cedar Grove customers moved into the new Springfield Heights development and asked us to follow them. Word got around, and within two years we had a standing weekly route through Springfield. Today, several of our 14 technicians live in Springfield full-time.

Springfield's housing mix runs a little different from Cedar Grove. Most of the newer subdivisions were built between 2012 and 2022 with modern SEER-rated equipment and reasonable ductwork — the early failures we see are typically condenser fan motors and outdoor capacitors baked by the Texas summer. The older homes closer to downtown are where we find the real work: oversized 1980s gas furnaces, undersized returns, and original single-stage AC units nearing the end of their life.

Springfield's climate is identical to Cedar Grove's — triple-digit summers, humid Gulf air, mild winters with occasional freeze events — so the sizing and equipment recommendations don't change city-to-city. What does change is the drive time. Calls from Springfield usually arrive in 45–60 minutes because we route the closest truck, and in several cases that truck is already parked at a tech's house in Springfield Heights.

Full AC replacements in Springfield typically range from $8,500 to $16,000 depending on tonnage, SEER2 rating, brand, and whether ductwork needs modification. Heat pumps generally run $9,000–$18,000.

Springfield homes trend slightly smaller than Cedar Grove, so 2.5–4 ton systems are more common than 5-ton installs. The biggest cost drivers are (1) tonnage, (2) efficiency — a base-tier 14.3 SEER2 unit costs meaningfully less than an 18+ SEER2 variable-speed system, though the premium unit usually pays back within 5–7 years — and (3) ductwork condition.
