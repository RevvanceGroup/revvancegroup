Common Questions

HVAC Questions Cedar Grove Homeowners Ask

Answers sourced from 17 years of real service calls. Don't see your question? Call us — we'll answer it in under 30 seconds.

A full HVAC system replacement in Cedar Grove typically ranges from $8,500 to $16,000 depending on system size, efficiency rating (SEER2), brand, and whether ductwork modifications are required. Heat pumps generally fall in the $9,000–$18,000 range.

The biggest cost drivers are: (1) tonnage — a 2-ton system for a 1,500 sq ft home runs less than a 5-ton system for a 3,500 sq ft home; (2) efficiency rating — a 14.3 SEER2 base unit is significantly cheaper than an 18+ SEER2 variable-speed system, though the premium unit pays back within 5–7 years through lower bills; (3) ductwork condition — if your existing ducts are leaking or undersized, repair costs $800–$2,500.

Meridian provides free in-home estimates with upfront fixed pricing — no commission-based upsells, no surprise charges.

Meridian offers same-day service Monday through Saturday for all repair calls placed before 2 PM. For true emergencies — no cooling during triple-digit heat or no heat during a freeze — we dispatch 24/7 with a typical arrival window of 60–90 minutes anywhere in Cedar Grove or surrounding cities.

We run 14 fully-stocked service trucks. When you call, we text you the assigned technician's name, photo, and live GPS arrival time so you know exactly who's coming and when.

A straight swap of an existing system (same size, same configuration) takes 6–8 hours and is completed in a single day. A full HVAC replacement including ductwork modifications or a fuel source change (gas to electric heat pump) runs 1.5–2 days.

On installation day: crew arrives at the scheduled time in uniform. Floors are covered with drop cloths. Old system is removed and hauled away at no charge. New system is installed per manufacturer spec. System is commissioned, tested, and we walk you through thermostat operation and filter changes before leaving. Permits and final inspection handled by us.

Our trucks stock approximately $15,000 in common failure parts — capacitors, contactors, blower motors, control boards, common refrigerants — so 90% of repairs are fixed in a single visit with no return trip.

SEER2 is the newer efficiency rating system that replaced SEER as of January 2023. SEER2 uses updated testing conditions that more accurately reflect real-world residential installations — specifically accounting for external static pressure from your home's ductwork.

As a rough conversion: a 14 SEER unit under the old system is approximately equivalent to a 13.4 SEER2 unit. The minimum efficiency for a new AC installed in Texas is now 14.3 SEER2. Higher-efficiency variable-speed systems can reach 20+ SEER2, cutting cooling costs by 40–50% compared to a 20-year-old unit.

The industry rule of thumb: if the repair cost exceeds 50% of the cost of a new system AND the unit is more than 10 years old, replacement is the better long-term investment.

Other signs it's time to replace: (1) your system uses R-22 refrigerant (phased out, very expensive to refill), (2) your electric bill has climbed steadily over the last 3–5 years despite similar usage, (3) some rooms are much hotter/colder than others, (4) you've had 2+ major repairs in the last 2 years.
