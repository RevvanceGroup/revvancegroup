Mini-Split / Ductless

The Most Efficient HVAC You Can Buy

Ductless mini-split systems are the highest-efficiency residential HVAC available, often 30-50% more efficient than central systems. Single-zone for one room, multi-zone for whole-home conversions, no ductwork required.

Single-Zone Mini-Splits

One outdoor unit, one indoor head. Ideal for a single room: home office, garage conversion, sunroom, primary bedroom. $3,800–$6,500 installed depending on capacity and brand.

Multi-Zone Systems

One outdoor unit handles 2-8 indoor heads. Each zone has independent temperature control and runs only when that room is occupied. Whole-home conversions for older homes without ductwork.

Up to 26 SEER2 Efficiency

Top-tier mini-splits hit 26 SEER2 cooling efficiency — nearly double the federal minimum for central systems. Operating cost is dramatically lower than a comparable conventional AC.

Quiet Operation

Indoor heads run at 19-30 dB — quieter than a whisper. Outdoor units are 50-60 dB, well below conventional condenser units. No more loud AC startup that wakes the house.

Heating + Cooling Combined

Most mini-splits are heat pumps — they heat AND cool. Even in cold weather, modern Hyper-Heat models maintain capacity down to -13°F. Replaces both AC and electric heat.

Federal Tax Credit Eligible

Energy Star certified mini-splits qualify for the federal $2,000 tax credit under the Inflation Reduction Act. We document the certification for your taxes.

When Mini-Split Makes Sense

The Best Use Cases

Home offices and garage conversions

Adding HVAC to a single room without extending ductwork is exactly what mini-splits do best. A 12,000 BTU single-zone system handles up to 600 sq ft and installs in a day.

Older homes without ductwork

If your historic Cedar Grove home has radiator or baseboard heat and window AC, a multi-zone mini-split conversion gives you whole-home cooling and modern heat without tearing into walls for ductwork.

Additions and ADUs

Bonus rooms, accessory dwelling units, and converted attics are often impossible to tie into the existing HVAC. Mini-splits provide independent heating and cooling on a dedicated circuit.

Hot/cold spots in central-system homes

Some rooms always run too hot or cold despite zoning attempts. Adding a single-zone mini-split to that room gives independent temperature control without rebalancing the entire system.

Server rooms and home gyms

Spaces with concentrated heat loads (server racks, exercise equipment) benefit from dedicated cooling that doesn’t affect the rest of the home.
